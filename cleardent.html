ClearDent Case Study

Project Summary

ClearDent is a dental practice management software provider based in Vancouver, with over 40% market shares in Western Canada. I have helped ClearDent to completely overhaul the company's website as an in-house design lead, working rigorously as a team with the Revenue (Sales & Marketing) department. 

Team Members
  • Chief Revenue Officer
  • Senior Marketing Specialist
  • Outsourced Marketing Consultant
  • Design Lead
Timeline

September - December 2018 (12 weeks)

My Role

As the design lead in the project, my primary responsibilities were in visual design, web development and project management. 

Problems & Challenges
  • Misalignment between content and the branding
  • Prominent user-flow/usability issues
  • Misaligned and outdated design elements
Goal

Design a website communicates the values of the company and its product with an user-centric approach

Starting with the big problem:
Restructuring the Information Architecture

Solutions vs Features

Our team have concluded that the website is heavily weighted on showcasing the line of products*,  but not enough on what 'solutions' can be offered to the specific needs of the potential buyers. As a software solutions provider, we were missing a big chunk.

Renaming the products/features

Because of the website simply displayed the product names, many of the users were not aware of what the product offers.

Creating the content focused website

With the company's new marketing strategy, the team needed to put the effort into making a content focused website.

Reorganizing the menu items

Some of the menu items, such as 'About us' and 'event' pages has been re-ordered based on its page view trends.

Typefaces

Futura PT for heading, Nunito for Body type; The classic font Futura was chosen for the typeface to communicate the boldness

Colors

Since the visual branding was not changing, and the management only preferred the incremental changes, I have saturated the original blend of primary and secondary color, Blue and Orange for its visibility and liveliness.

Design Guidelines

The design system was created for the internal use for both Marketing and R&D team's use, and the system was being actively used for the new web app development which I was lucky to be a small part of in their deisgn initiative.
